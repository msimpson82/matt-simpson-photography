A Single Headshot Can Make A Huge Impact.

Like it or not, we're in the swipe-right era, where we navigate our professional and social landscapes by being seen first online, long before meeting in person.

Whether it's LinkedIn, email signatures, online bios, or dating profiles, we are at the mercy of the mobile snap judgment. So put on a happy face and get ready for your close-up. It may be the first (and last) impression you can make.

My Two Goals For Every Headshot

Personally, I like to identify two key attributes I want my professional headshots to convey for clients.

1

Confidence

Your headshot should radiate competence and self-assurance. This is someone who can get the job done.

2

Approachability

You want to appear warm and accessible. This is someone I can talk to and work with.

Call it my North Star of headshot photography. When your potential client or hiring manager sees your profile picture, I want their first impression to be: a) this is someone I can talk to, and b) this is someone who can get the job done. With those two pillars guiding our shoot, I am confident we can leave the session with something that will empower you and leave you feeling ready to land that next job.
